About

Science
rooted in
the wild

I am a Molecular Ecologist with an MSc from the University of Innsbruck, specialising in biodiversity research, DNA-based monitoring, and population genetics. My work spans alpine wetlands, insect rearing facilities, and conservation reserves — bridging laboratory precision with field intuition.

My master's research uncovered the population structure of Lissotriton vulgaris (Smooth Newts) across North Tyrol, using non-invasive sampling and microsatellite analysis to inform conservation priorities. That same curiosity about life's molecular architecture carried into published work on the Black Soldier Fly microbiome.

Currently based between Europe and the USA, I bring an international perspective and a deep commitment to environmental stewardship — in the lab, in the field, and in the classroom.
